How to Change a Flat Tyre

The following procedure makes some assumptions:

  • You are parked safely and on a flat surface so that you are not a danger to yourself or others while changing the tyre.
  • You have all the necessary equipment to carry out the tyre change.
    • A jack
    • A lug wrench
    • A fully inflated spare tyre
    • The car's owners manual

Step 1

  1. Apply the parking brake.
  2. Turn on your Hazard Lights.
  3. Remove the Jack, Lug Wrench, and Spare Tyre from the boot of your car.

Step 2

  1. Use the lug wrench to loosen the lug nuts.
    • You might need to remove hubcaps before attempting this.
    • Note: Only loosen the lug nuts. Do not remove them.

Step 3

  1. Position the jack under the car at the correct point on the frame
    • Note: You might need the car's owners manual to locate where to position the jack.
  2. Raise the jack until the tyre you are changing is 5 to 6 inches clear of the ground.
    • Leave enough clearance for the fully inflated spare tyre to be put on.

Step 4

  1. Unscrew the lug nuts removing them from the wheel
  2. Pull the tyre gently and straight towards you until it is completely free of the hub. Lie it flat to one side.

Step 5

  1. To put on the spare tyre, place it directly in front of the wheelarch.
  2. Lift up the spare trye, lining up the rim with the lug bolts. Push the lug bolt gently through the hole on the rim.
  3. Once the threads on the lug bolts are fully shown, hand tighten the lug nuts to the lug bolts.
  4. Using the jack, lower the car, but not fully, so the full weight of the car is not on the tyre.
  5. Take the lug wrench to tighten the lug nuts fully.
    • Once the lug nuts have been tightened by the wrench, use the full weight of your body to turn them to be as fully tightened as possible.
  6. Use the jack to completely lower the car.
    • Using the lug wrench, tightened the lug nuts one more time.
